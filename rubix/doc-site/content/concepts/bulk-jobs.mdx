# Bulk & Jobs

A spine for work that may not finish inside one request: bulk writes and wide streaming
reads promote to **background jobs** with a ticketed WebSocket channel for progress.

## Tiered execution

- **Tier 1 (synchronous).** `POST /records/bulk` runs each item through the gate with
  per-item isolation and returns `200` if it finishes by a soft deadline (~10s).
- **Tier 2 (promoted).** If the deadline passes — or for `POST /query` with
  `stream: true` — the request returns `202` with a **job id** and keeps running in the
  background.

## Job tickets & registry

A **job ticket** is a short-TTL (~15 min), hashed-at-rest bearer credential, scoped
narrower than a session token. A live job lives in an in-memory **registry** with
per-principal and per-namespace caps (over the cap → `429`). Terminal jobs are evicted
after a grace window by a background sweeper, which also reaps expired tickets.

## Progress channel

`GET /ws/jobs/{id}` streams typed frames (`Item` / `Chunk` / `Done` / `Failed`). The
ticket is presented via the WebSocket **subprotocol** (browser-friendly, not a query
string). A backlog ring replays recent frames to a late or reconnecting subscriber, then
tails the live broadcast until terminal.

## Polling & cancel

- `GET /bulk/jobs/{id}` — status always; **poll-transport** jobs (bulk CRUD) also return
  the buffered per-item result; **stream-transport** jobs (queries) are status-only with
  a hint to consume the WS.
- `DELETE /bulk/jobs/{id}` — cancels and eagerly revokes the ticket.

After a restart the registry is empty, so an orphan ticket resolves to `404` ("job
unknown"), not an auth error — a safe failure. The full design lives in the internal
`BULK-AND-JOBS.md` spec.
